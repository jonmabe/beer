# Half Blind Raven Table Saison v2

**Style:** Table Saison | **ABV:** ~4.0–4.3% | **IBU:** ~20-22 | **Batch Size:** 5 gal (Ss Brewtech SVBS)

## The Vision

> I want a lower ABV table saison that lives somewhere between Hitachino Nest Saison du Japon
> and Saison Dupont — a little hoppy, but the mouthfeel isn't like a lager. It's more like a red ale
> mouthfeel. Not the flavor, just the thickness of the liquid.

The challenge: table saisons want to be thin and dry, but this one needs to drink bigger than its gravity. Instead of stacking body ingredients, we're using a high mash temp to leave unfermentable dextrins, a structured fermentation ramp to generate glycerol and phenols (perceived body), and a small sugar addition to boost ester and phenol production. The beer should finish dry but feel substantial — peppery, lively, and complex enough that you don't miss the gravity.

---

## v1 Lessons (July 2024)

The first attempt used Mosaic and Galaxy hops (too fruity), mashed at 149°F (too fermentable), hit only 2.4 volumes CO₂ (too flat), and fermented with Belle Saison. The beer was thin, fruity, and uninteresting. Three gallons also ended up on the garage floor due to an open valve, so the sample size was limited. Every decision in v2 is designed to correct those problems.

## Water

### Why Not Tap

Anaheim tap water (2025 Water Quality Report) is the wrong profile for this beer:

| Parameter | Anaheim Tap (avg) | Our Target | Problem |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sulfate | 135 ppm | ~80 ppm | Way too high — accentuates bitterness, thins mouthfeel |
| Chloride | 89 ppm | ~120 ppm | Too low — we need chloride-forward for body |
| Total Hardness | 318 ppm (CaCO3) | Much lower | Very hard water |
| Calcium | 97 ppm | ~50-70 ppm | High |
| Total Alkalinity | 180 ppm (CaCO3) | Much lower | Will push mash pH too high for a pilsner-heavy bill |

The water is sulfate-forward — great for IPAs, terrible for what we're doing. Trying to dilute and correct is more hassle than it's worth.

### The Move: RO Water + Brewing Salts

1. **Fill two empty corny kegs with RO water** from a grocery store fill station (~$0.25-0.35/gal). Need ~8 gallons total.
2. **Add brewing salts** to build the profile from scratch:

| Salt | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Calcium Chloride (CaCl₂) | Bring chloride to ~120 ppm, adds calcium |
| Gypsum (CaSO₄) — small amount | Bring sulfate to ~80 ppm |
| Calcium Carbonate (CaCO₃) — if needed | Mash pH adjustment |

3. **Check mash pH** — target 5.2–5.4. With RO water and a pilsner-heavy grain bill, you may need a small acid addition (lactic acid) or rely on the acidulated character of the malt. Use a pH meter or strips on brew day.

Pick up brewing salts from Windsor (or confirm they're in stock).

## Brew Day (SVBS)

### Volumes

| Step | Volume |
|---|---|
| Mash water | ~4.5 gal (2 qt/lb) |
| Sparge | ~3.5 gal |
| Pre-boil | ~6.5 gal |
| Post-boil (into fermenter) | ~5.25 gal |

The SVBS is a 12 gal vessel designed for 5 gal batches — plenty of headroom. Adjust volumes for your system's actual dead space and boil-off rate.

### Mash

- **Single infusion at 156°F for 60 min**
- Use the SVBS controller to set and hold temp — recirculate the entire time
- This is deliberately high — leaving dextrins behind that the yeast can't fully break down
- No step mash needed

### Boil

- 60 min total
- Hop, spice, and sugar additions per schedule above
- 0.4 lb white cane sugar goes in at 10 min

## Fermentation — Structured Ramp

This is a two-phase approach borrowed from how Dupont and Blaugies handle their saisons. The slow start builds clean fermentation character; the hot finish generates glycerol, phenols, and esters that create perceived body in a low-gravity beer.

### Phase 1 — Clean Start (48 hours)

- Pitch at **64–66°F**
- Hold for 48 hours
- Yeast builds healthy cell walls, ferments simple sugars cleanly
- This prevents the thin "homebrew saison" character

### Phase 2 — Farmhouse Ramp

Starting at hour 48, increase **3–4°F per day:**

| Day | Target Temp |
|---|---|
| Pitch | 66°F |
| Day 3 | 70°F |
| Day 4 | 74°F |
| Day 5 | 78°F |
| Day 6 | 82°F |
| Day 7 | 86°F |
| Day 8+ | 90°F — hold here |

### Finishing

- 3724 will likely stall around 1.020–1.025 — this is normal, the heat pushes it through
- Check gravity every few days starting week 2
- Expecting terminal gravity of **~1.006–1.008** (sugar addition helps attenuation)
- Two identical readings 48 hours apart = done
- Cold crash optional (clarity isn't critical for saison)
- **Total fermentation: ~3–4 weeks**

---

## Kegging & Carbonation

**Target: 3.0–3.2 volumes CO₂**

- Force carb at **~20-22 PSI at 38°F** for about a week, or use the shake method for 1-2 days
- High carbonation is non-negotiable for saison — it transforms mouthfeel from "thin table beer" to "lively farmhouse ale"
- **Serving note:** 3.0+ volumes requires longer beer lines (8-10 ft) or reduced serving pressure (~12 PSI) to avoid foam

**Packaging to pour-ready: ~5 weeks from brew day**

## Expected Character

- **Look:** Pale gold, slight haze from wheat proteins
- **Nose:** Pepper, light citrus fruit, subtle orange, floral hop
- **Taste:** Dry but not hollow — earthy spice, hint of coriander, gentle hop bitterness
- **Mouthfeel:** Medium body for the gravity, lively carbonation, drinks like a 5%+ beer
- **Finish:** Peppery, dry, refreshing

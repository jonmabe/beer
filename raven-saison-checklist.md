### Water Chemistry — Calculate Salt Additions
- [ ] Use **Bru'n Water** or **Brewfather** water calculator
- [ ] Input: distilled/RO water (all zeros) as your source
- [ ] Target profile:
  - Calcium: ~50–70 ppm
  - Chloride: ~120 ppm
  - Sulfate: ~80 ppm
  - Chloride-to-sulfate ratio: ~1.5:1 (malt-forward)
- [ ] Calculate grams of CaCl₂ needed for **full volume** (mash + sparge)
- [ ] Calculate grams of gypsum needed for **full volume**
- [ ] Write down the amounts — you'll split between mash water and sparge water
- [ ] Note: salts go into water BEFORE grain

## ☀️ Saturday — Brew Day

### Pre-Brew (8:30 AM)
- [ ] Set M29 yeast packet on counter (room temp, 30 min before pitch)
- [ ] Zest **1 orange** — no pith (white part), just the outer skin
- [ ] Measure out **0.4 lb white cane sugar** into a bowl
- [ ] Lay out all hop/spice additions in order with labels
- [ ] Fill a sink or bucket with **Star San** for sanitizing tools

### Water & Salts (8:45 AM)
- [ ] Pour **~4.5 gal distilled water** into SVBS (mash water)
- [ ] Add calculated **CaCl₂** to mash water — stir to dissolve
- [ ] Add calculated **gypsum** to mash water — stir to dissolve
- [ ] Prepare **~3.5 gal sparge water** separately with proportional salts
- [ ] Start heating mash water to **~160°F** strike temp (overshoots to hit 156°F with grain)

### Mash (9:00 AM)
- [ ] **Dough in** — slowly add grain to mash water, stir to avoid clumps
- [ ] Confirm mash temp settles at **156°F**
- [ ] Start SVBS **recirculation**
- [ ] **Check mash pH** at 10–15 minutes:
  - Target: **5.2–5.4**
  - If too high: add **lactic acid** (a few drops at a time, re-check)
  - If too low (unlikely with distilled): no action needed
- [ ] Mash for **60 minutes** total
- [ ] Heat sparge water to **168–170°F** during last 15 min of mash

### Sparge (10:00 AM)
- [ ] Stop recirculation
- [ ] Begin sparging with **~3.5 gal water at 168–170°F**
- [ ] Collect wort — target **~6.5 gal pre-boil volume**
- [ ] Note pre-boil gravity if you have a refractometer (sanity check)

### Boil (10:20 AM — 60 min total)
- [ ] Bring to **rolling boil** — watch for hot break (foam), stir through it
- [ ] **60 min:** Add **0.5 oz Styrian Goldings** ← start timer here
- [ ] **10 min** (50 min into boil): Add **0.5 oz Hallertau Blanc** + **0.4 lb sugar**
- [ ] **5 min** (55 min into boil): Add **crushed coriander** + **orange zest**
- [ ] **0 min:** Flame out / kill heat

### Chill & Transfer (11:20 AM)
- [ ] Start **wort chiller** — chill to **68°F** as fast as possible
- [ ] While chilling: sanitize fermenter, airlock, any transfer equipment
- [ ] Take **OG reading** — target **~1.040**
  - Record actual OG: ______
- [ ] Transfer wort to fermenter
- [ ] **Oxygenate** — shake fermenter vigorously for 60 seconds (or use O2 stone)

### Pitch (11:45 AM-ish)
- [ ] Confirm wort is at **68°F**
- [ ] Open **M29 packet**, sprinkle on top of wort
- [ ] Seal fermenter, attach **airlock or blow-off tube**
- [ ] Set fermentation temp to **68°F**
- [ ] **Do not disturb for 48 hours**

### Clean Up
- [ ] Clean SVBS immediately (easier when warm)
- [ ] Rinse wort chiller
- [ ] Clean all tools, paddles, measuring equipment
- [ ] Dump spent grain (compost or trash)

## 🍺 Kegging & Carbonation (~Week 3–4)

- [ ] Confirm FG is stable (two readings match)
- [ ] Cold crash fermenter to **38°F** for 24–48 hours (optional, for clarity)
- [ ] **Sanitize keg** — fill with Star San, push out with CO₂
- [ ] Transfer beer to keg — closed transfer if possible to minimize oxidation
- [ ] Set **CO₂ to 20–22 PSI at 38°F**
- [ ] Target: **3.0–3.2 volumes CO₂**
- [ ] Force carb for **5–7 days** (or shake method for 1–2 days)
- [ ] After carb: reduce serving pressure to **~12 PSI**
- [ ] Use **8–10 ft beer lines** to handle high carbonation without foam
